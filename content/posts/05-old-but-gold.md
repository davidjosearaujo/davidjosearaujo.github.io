# Amazing is simple

The most practical tools are not complex, they are practical precisely because they are easy to use, they are there when you need them and don't make you question yourself about if have the skills to use them or you're just a dumbass.

Linux through out its history as collected a huge amount of this amazing tools, such as `file`, `diff`, `grep`, `cat`, `strings` and so many others. I could spend the day going through their `man` pages. Each of this is cleverly simple and limited to a specific use and purpose. Like a chef's knife is awful at cutting pretty much anything other that food, these tools don't doo much, but what they do, they do it perfectly!

And that it way they are amazing.

When you want to read the content of the file you just "`cat` it". You don't care about the format of the encoding of indentation, you just look inside, and you don't need a space suit for the first floor!
